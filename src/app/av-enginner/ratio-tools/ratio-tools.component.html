What It Is

The width and height of an image have a fixed ratio, called the aspect ratio. It is written as x:y, where x and y are two numbers. For instance, an image with a 16:9 aspect ratio will always have 16 units of width for every 9 units of height, regardless of its size. So, both an image that is 16 cm wide and 9 cm high and an image that is 32 cm wide and 18 cm high have the same 16:9 aspect ratio.

Aspect ratio Formula
The formula for finding aspect ratio is: AR=W/H

Where:

  • AR is the aspect ratio

  • W is the width of the displayed image

  • H is the height of the displayed image

Note that W and H are measured in inches or millimeters.

The diagonal of a screen can be calculated using the Pythagorean Theorem: A2 + B2 = C2

Estimating Throw Distance Formula
The formula for estimating throw is: D = SW * TR

Where:

  • D is the distane from the front of the lens to the closest point on the screen

  • SW is screen width or width of the project image

  • TR is the throw ratio or ratio of throw distance to image width
